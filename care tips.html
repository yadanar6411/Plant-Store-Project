Care guide about plants

HCY

Aloe Vera

Aloe Vera

Thrives in bright, indirect sunlight. Use well-draining soil and water only when the soil is dry.

Boston Fern

Boston Fern

Prefers bright, indirect light. Keep soil evenly moist and provide high humidity for best growth.

Weeping fig

Weeping fig

Needs bright, indirect light. Water when the top soil dries and avoid sudden temperature changes.

Cactus

Cactus

Requires bright sunlight and sandy, well-draining soil. Water sparingly and allow soil to dry fully between watering.

Pine Plant

Pine Plant

Thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. Water deeply but infrequently once established.

Golden Pothos

Golden Pothos

Thrives in bright, indirect light. Keep soil consistently moist but avoid waterlogging.

Peace Lily

Peace Lily

Thrives in bright, indirect light. Keep soil consistently moist but avoid overwatering.

Snake Plant

Snake Plant

Tolerates low to bright indirect light. Water only when the soil is completely dry.

Date Plant

Date Plant

Needs bright sunlight and well-draining soil. Water deeply when the top layer dries out.

Syngonium (pink) Plant

Syngonium (pink) Plant

Thrives in bright, indirect light. Keep soil lightly moist and avoid overwatering.

Holy Basil

Holy Basil

Prefers full sun and fertile, well-draining soil. Keep soil slightly moist.

Peepal Tree

Peepal Tree

Grows best in full sun. Water young plants regularly; mature trees are drought-tolerant.

English Ivy

English Ivy

Prefers bright, indirect light. Keep soil slightly moist and maintain moderate humidity.

Hens and Chicks

Hens and Chicks

Needs full sun and sandy, well-draining soil. Water sparingly and allow soil to dry between watering.

Areca Palm

Areca Palm

Grows well in bright, indirect light. Water when top soil dries and keep humidity moderate.

Rubber Plant

Rubber Plant

Thrives in bright, indirect light. Water when the top soil dries out and avoid cold drafts.

Fun facts about plant care

  • Too much sun can burn leaves—plants need to adjust slowly.
  • Overwatering is the plant killer, not underwatering.
  • Roots need air—soggy soil suffocates them.
  • Plants send chemical “warning signals” to each other.
  • Some flowers bloom only at night.


Fun facts about plant care

  • Plants bend toward light (phototropism).
  • Clean leaves = better growth.
  • Temperature shock can make leaves drop.
  • Plants have an internal clock.
  • They respond to vibrations, not music.
